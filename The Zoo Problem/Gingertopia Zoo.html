Zoo Animals

Bears

  • Ollie
    • A fuzzy boi with a big temper. Feeding time is 6AM, 12PM and 6PM. Miss any of them and Ollie will go feral. He only accepts pets if he has been fed, otherwise your arm will be a snack.
  • Mona
    • The sweetest girl that loves to nap. To her every season is hibernation season. She is just like Ollie when it comes to being fed, although she will get far more violent if a feeding time is missed.
    Bear Facts

    Giraffes

  • Frankie
    • This animal is one of a kind and has a very chaotic personality. This guy once took a hat off a vistor and tossed it to Coconut. The hat was not recovered.
  • Coconut
    • Nobody knows who named a giraffe Coconut, but strangely it's pretty fitting. We don't know how he keeps doing it, but Coconut keeps finding himself in high places where he will intentionally fall. We think he does this for attention.
    Giraffe Facts

    Lions

  • Mella
    • The star of the whole zoo! Mella is our most frequently visited animal at the zoo and she loves the attention! Mella can often be seen interacting with the little kids that get up close to the protective glass.
  • Karl
    • The sassiest lion we have ever rescued by a long mile. Visitor love coming to see Karl because there is no telling what he could be up to on any given day. One day he was yeeting himself into the pond repeatedly and the next day he was playing fetch by himself with some stray branches. We love Karl.
    Lion Facts

    Monkeys

  • Cookie
    • This little rascal is an escape artist and a damn good one at that. Several times a day we have to chase Cookie around to get him back in the enclosure, but have you tried catching a wild monkey? It's near impossible.
  • Earl
    • Much like Karl the Lion, Earl is a wildcard and is the most erratic animal in the entire zoo. We have had numerous reports of Earl yeeting bananas at people then screaming like a banshee.
  • Banana Pudding
    • We think whoever named Coconut probably named this monkey. Probably the most beloved monkey in the zoo, Banana Pudding is the calmest animal we have here. She doesn't do much, but on numerous occassions we have seen her teaching sign language to kids.
    Monkey Facts

    Alligators

  • Wren
    • We here at Gingertopia Zoo have a soft spot for the Alligators and Wren is one of our personal favorites. Extremely docile and calm, Wren loves to cuddle up in the laps of our Zoo Pros and take a nap. She once napped with a Zoo Pro for 4 hours without so much and stirring.
  • Aspen
    • Much like Wren, Aspen loves to nap but she prefers a nice shady spot underneath a tree. While Aspen is extremely friendly to all guests and Zoo Pros, she prefers to spend her time teasing Mika or observing the visitors from underneath her favorite tree.
  • Mika
    • Probably the friendliest animal in the entire Zoo, Mika loves to get up close and personal with visitors and will sometimes let children pet her. One of Mika's favorite activities is to greet the visitors face-to-face, with Zoo Pros supervising of course.
